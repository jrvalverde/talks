2 ABOUT THE HGMP-RC

2.1 WHAT WE DO

The HGMP-RC is a body funded by the UK Medical Research Council to provide Bioinformatics and Biological services to the UK academic community working on the Human Genome Programme and to carry out research in its own right in a newly formed research division. In practice, the HGMP-RC currently provides a free service to any bona fide academic who is interested.To use most of our biological and computing resources, you must register as a user.There is currently a fee of Â£ 5000 per annum to register a set of users from a commercial company.

2.2 MISSION STATEMENT

  • To provide both biological and data resources and services to the medical research community, with a special emphasis on those relevant to the Human Genome Programme.
  • To facilitate genomic research by the provision of cost effective centralised collaborative and training facilities.
  • To encourage users to share their data, information and resources.
  • To encourage the transfer of technology from the academic to commercial/industrial applications.

2.3 BIOINFORMATICS

Users can store their data files on our computer system. We take security very seriously. We provide a menu system allowing users to run programs on their data or browse genomic and other databases. We have an extensive list of links to useful external www sites.

2.5 RESEARCH

We have some biology and computing research groups. They are working on projects like sequencing part of mouse chromosome X, landmark mapping of the Fugu genome, computer prediction of transcription factors, databases of sources of biological materials, etc.

2.6 TRAINING COURSES

We run a set of training courses covering the use of bioinformatics programs and techniques at both an introductory and a specialised level. Up to date information can be found at:http://www.hgmp.mrc.ac.uk/About/Courses/
