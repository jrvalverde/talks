8 FURTHER PROJECTS

The aim of these exercises is for people to work through and use the various concepts and computing tools you have learnt over the last few days. It should help to put these into context and also expand your knowledge boundaries of what is possible. We will break every so often to discuss what people have done and what other types of analysis or data searches could be done. There is also a small write up on phylogeny.If people want to work on their own sequences and projects this is an ideal time to do so, and should give you a head start for when you go back to your lab.

8.1 WHAT CAN YOU FIND OUT ABOUT A HUMAN SNAIL GENE?

  1. What do the human nucleotide and SwissProt sequences look like?
  2. What about in other organisms?
  3. Do you find more putative representatives if you search the EST sections? Can you find possible distant relatives at the protein level?
  4. Where does this occur in the human genome? If it is not documented, how in theory could this be predicted?
  5. Are there any interesting protein domains?
  6. What type of function could you predict?
  7. When would you expect this gene to be expressed?
  8. What genes are up and down stream of this gene?
  9. Can you find syntenic regions in other organisms?
  10. What is known about the clinical genetics? (OMIM and literature)
  11. How do the sequences vary? (At the nucleotide and protein levels).
  • Multiple Sequence alignment.
  • What types of phylogenetic analyses would be useful?

8.2 WHAT CAN YOU FIND OUT ABOUT THE PARENTAL GENE OF THE SMALL EST YOU HAVE SEQUENCED?

How could you look at the structure and determine possible functions?The sequence is in a file on our anonymous ftp server ( ftp.hgmp.mrc.ac.uk) in the /pub/course/small.est directory.Look at the bioinformatics overview chart for some initial suggestions.
  1. What proteins and nucleic acid sequences does it match to?
  2. How could you improve the alignment?
  3. What disease could a malfunction of its parental gene cause?
  4. What is the human chromosomal location?
  5. Is there a known homologous structure?
  6. Could you suggest a function?
